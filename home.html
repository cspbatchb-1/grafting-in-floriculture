GRAFTING IN FLOURICULTURE

Introduction to grafting

Grafting is a horicultural technique used to join partsfrom two or more plants so they grow as one. this methode is commonly employed to propagate trees and shrubs,especially those that do not root well from cuttings.Grafting involves taking a place of one plant,called the scion ,and attaching it to another plant ,kno as the rootstock. the scion become the top part of the plant,producing leaves,flowers,and fruits,while the rootstockfrom the root system and sometimes the lower paer of the system.the two parts must be closely related botanically for the graft to take.

Types of grafting

1.whip Grafting
2.cleft Grafting
3.Bud Grafting
4.Approach grafting

1.whip grafting:Used for joining small plants or branches of similar size.it involves making matching sloping cuts on the scion and rootstock and securing them together

2.cleft grafting:used for joining a small scion to a larger rootstock.A cleft is made in the rootstock,and the scion is insertd into the cleft.

3.Bud grafting:Inolves inserting a bud from the scion into a slit made in the bark of the rootstock.

4Approch grafting:Both scion and rootstock remain rooted and growing until the graft is secure, after which one of them is cut away.

Floriculture is a division of ornamental horticulture that deals with growing, selling, and arranging flower and ornamental plants. Floriculture is largely thought of as a greenhouse industry because flowers and potted plants are largely grown in plant-growing systems in temperate climates, though certain flowers are cultivated outdoors in nurseries or crop fields. Floriculture meaning includes both the cultivation of bedding plants and the production of cuttings for growing in greenhouses or for use as houseplants indoors.Floriculture is the cultivation and trade of flowers. It encompasses flower production, processing, marketing, and distribution. The first recorded evidence of Floriculture dates back to the early 1500s when flowers were grown in greenhouses in Holland. Today, Floriculture is a multibillion-dollar industry with a global reach. In the United States, Floriculture is a nearly $20 billion industry, with California being the top producer of flowers. The Netherlands is the world's largest exporter of flowers, followed by Germany and Italy.
